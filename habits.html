Habits

  1. Shooting Game or FPS Game
    Action video games in the first person shooter (FPS) genre are played from the viewpoint of the main character.
    FPS games frequently simulate an actual player's motions and give a glimpse of what they would see and do in-game.

  2. Role-playing (RPG)
    These games cover a wide range of titles where the player creates or assumes control of a character and can progress through leveling up by gaining experience points.
    A unifying theme and a clear tale are two features that RPGs frequently share.

  3. Multiplayer online battle arena (MOBA)
    To vanquish opponents in a fight is the only objective of the strategy role-playing game genre known as MOBA (multiplayer online battle arena).
    In contrast to the strategy genre, each MOBA player only has control over one hero and participates in the team's general plan.

  4. Real-time strategy (RTS)
    A time-based computer game known as real-time strategy (RTS) is focused on using resources to create units and fight enemies.
    When compared to turn-based strategy games, where each player has time to carefully contemplate his or her next move without having to worry about the opponent's actions,
    real-time strategy games are frequently described as being more strategic.
    Real-time strategy games require players to accumulate resources, protect their bases, and launch attacks while while being aware of the opponent's frantic efforts to accomplish the same tasks.

  5. Puzzlers and party games
    Due to the emphasis on game mechanics in both of these games, there is some major overlap. These games will typically include a theme, a narrative, or be modeled after a classic board game.
    Party games frequently feature multiplayer and increase gameplay. Games with puzzles are puzzlers.

  6. Action-adventure
    Since it is a hybrid genre, action-adventure games often fall into other, more specific categories. Pure adventure games typically include the user solving situational puzzles with little to no action.
    If there is action, it usually only occurs during individual minigames. The gameplay in pure action games is built on in-the-moment interactions that test the player's reflexes.
    As a result, both problem-solving and reflexes are used in action-adventure games in both violent and non-violent settings.
